Gauss Partial Pivot

  • This method is applied to matrices seeking to operate with them making transformations such as the interchange of rows and columns, perform mathematical operations such as multiplications or divisions in the rows or columns by constants looking in the lower triangle to leave everything at zero in the ideal and operate as far as possible to determine if the matrix has a single solution, multiple solutions or no solution.
  • A main row should be taken to perform the operations looking to convert all the elements of the first element to 0, this main row being at the top of the array.
  • Once the elements below the row of the initial element are zero, we proceed to take the second row as the main row, we operate until everything below the first element of the main diagonal is zero repeating this process.
  • If there is 0 in the main diagonal, it cannot be operated, so it is necessary to rearrange or rows or columns to correct truncation problems.
  • The Algorithm seeks to rearrange the rows and columns in such a way that values other than 0 are on the main diagonal, and then perform the simple Gaussian elimination method and reduce rounding errors.
  • In the matrix, its upper triangle must have a system of equations equivalent to the initial one. In general this method proposes the elimination of variables from the two-dimensional system, this method is effective for simple operations.
