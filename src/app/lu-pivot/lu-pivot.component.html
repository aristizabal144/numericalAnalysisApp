LU Pivot

  • It works as a base from LU Simple but now with row permutations.
  • The L matrix its built with the multipliers located according their respective index and with ones on the diagonal.
  • We have PA = LU where L and U are again lower and upper triangular matrices, and now P is a permutation matrix, which, when left-multiplied to A, reorders the rows of A.
  • It turns out that all square matrices can be factorized in this form, and the factorization is numerically stable in practice.
  • This makes LUP decomposition a useful technique in practice.
