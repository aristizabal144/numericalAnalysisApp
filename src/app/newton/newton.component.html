Newton Method

  • Newton's method serves us in different ways, such as: a variant of a fixed point that defines it as a function g (x).
  • Dispersion arguments can be given because the process is suspended when the relative or absolute error is less than the tolerance
  • Precision arguments can be given because it can be argued based on intermediate value theorem, that the resulting approximation is near the exact root of the equation.
  • The method must have a number of iterations, generated approximations, function and error, additional to this the derivated function.
  • Two good results can be found, the first is because the root was found and the second because a minor error to tolerance occurred.
  • The derivative must be different from 0 for its entire interval and must be continuous
  • Although Newton's method does not meet the ideal conditions for Interval methods, in some cases a proper analysis of the results of the application of the method can be achieved.
