Secant Method

  • The secant method is a root-finding algorithm that uses a succession of roots of secant lines to better approximate a root of a function f.
  • In general, the secant method is not guaranteed to converge towards a root, but under some conditions, it does.
  • This method can be thought of as a finite-difference approximation of Newton's method.
  • The method requires two initial values X0 and X1, which should ideally be chosen to lie close to the root
  • The secant method can be interpreted as a method in which the derivative is replaced by an approximation and is thus a quasi-Newton method.
  • If we compare Newton's method with the secant method, we see that Newton's method converges faster (order 2 against φ ≈ 1.6).
